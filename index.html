What Is a CCTV Camera?

A CCTV (Closed-Circuit Television) camera is a surveillance device designed to monitor and record activities within a specific area. Unlike broadcast television, CCTV footage is transmitted to a closed system, ensuring controlled access and enhanced security.

  • CCTV stands for Closed-Circuit Television.
  • It is used for monitoring people, property, and assets.
  • Footage can be viewed live or recorded.
  • Commonly used in homes, offices, shops, and public places.
  • Helps deter crime and unauthorized access.
  • Provides evidence for investigations.
  • Modern CCTV uses IP and cloud technology.
  • Supports mobile and remote monitoring.
  • Works 24/7 with night vision features.
  • Widely adopted in Dubai for safety compliance.

How CCTV Camera Systems Work

CCTV systems work by capturing video through cameras and transmitting it to recording devices or cloud servers. Users can access footage in real time or playback recorded video using monitors or mobile apps.

  • Camera captures video continuously or on motion.
  • Video is sent to DVR or NVR.
  • IP cameras use internet connectivity.
  • Footage is stored locally or on cloud.
  • Users can view footage remotely.
  • Mobile apps enable alerts and notifications.
  • Night vision ensures visibility in darkness.
  • AI detects motion and intrusion.
  • Playback helps in reviewing incidents.
  • System works 24 hours without interruption.
